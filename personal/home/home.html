What to Know About ADHD

What is ADHD

ADHD is a mental health diagnosis that is becoming more prevalent in our modern world. ADHD stands for Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder. ADHD consists of two major types, one that is more centered around attention deficits and one that is centered around hyperactivity. These can be seen together or separately. Attention deficits are most commonly recognized when someone seems to have a hard time focusing on tasks. Hyperactivity is more easily identified as it is more often an outward expression of energy. These are, however, scales in severity, and vary in the presentation depending on the person.

How to Identify ADHD

Now that we know what it is, let's talk more about what it looks like. ADHD can be attention deficits, hyperactivity, or a combination of the two. For it to be an ADHD diagnosis a few things have to be true. These issues need to have been present before the age of 12 and the impact of these things needs to be substantial. What makes the impact substantial? The answer varies, but it needs to be impacting your life and ability to function in some life, work, school, or social aspects.

Treatments for ADHD

ADHD, like all mental health diagnoses, has a number of options for treatment. However, there is one type of medication that is much more commonly used due to its higher success rate. The types of medications available fit into different categories depending on the way they react in the body. The ones that are used the vast majority of the time with ADHD are stimulants. Another common option is therapy, as it can be very beneficial to work with someone to create habits and plans to deal with the inattentiveness, the impulsiveness, and the hyperactivity so that one can better participate in the work they are required to do.
